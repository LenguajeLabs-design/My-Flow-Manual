Start Here

The iPad gestures worth learning first

These are the moves that make the biggest difference the fastest, especially if you want the iPad to feel more productive and less like a giant phone.

Go Home

Swipe up from bottom

Exit any app quickly without hunting for a button.

Think: one clean upward sweep resets your view.

App Switcher

Swipe up and hold

See recent apps and spaces so you can jump around faster.

Up and pause means “show me everything I was doing.”

Switch between recent apps

Swipe left or right on bottom bar

Quickly bounce between the apps you just used.

Use it like a tiny track beneath your work.

Open Control Center

Swipe down from top-right

Brightness, volume, Wi-Fi, rotation lock, and Focus are right there.

Top-right is the “controls” corner.

Open Notification Center

Swipe down from top-center

Check alerts without leaving your app entirely.

Top center is where your incoming stuff lives.

Spotlight search

Swipe down on Home Screen

Launch apps, find files, notes, messages, and settings instantly.

If you are hunting, search first.

Multitasking

The iPad features that unlock real work

These are the moves that make the iPad feel much more capable for writing, planning, browsing, or studying.

Use Split View for pairs that belong together

Good combos: Safari and Notes, Messages and Calendar, Files and Pages, or YouTube and Notes for learning.

Use Slide Over for short visits

Keep one smaller app floating for quick checks, messages, or reference material without replacing your main app.

Drag and drop between apps

You can drag text, links, images, and files from one app to another, which is one of the most useful iPad habits to build.

Files matters more than people think

Once you start saving things intentionally in Files, the iPad becomes much easier to treat like a real work device.

Editing

Touch tricks that make typing and editing less annoying

These are the things that make text work feel smoother whether you are taking notes, writing, or cleaning things up.

Pinch with three fingers to copy.

A small pinch-in gesture copies selected text or content.

Spread with three fingers to paste.

The reverse gesture pastes, which can feel surprisingly quick once it clicks.

Swipe left with three fingers to undo.

Great when you type or delete something by mistake and do not want to shake the device or hunt menus.

Swipe right with three fingers to redo.

Pairs naturally with undo and is worth learning as a set.

Turn the keyboard into a trackpad.

Press and hold the space bar to move the text cursor more precisely when typing feels clumsy.

Select text by dragging the cursor handles.

It is still one of the simplest ways to get clean text selection on iPad.

Use Scribble if you write with Pencil.

Writing directly into text fields can be faster than switching modes when you are thinking visually.

Setup Wins

The settings and habits that pay off most

A few small choices make the iPad more pleasant and far more practical long term.

Put your best tools in the Dock

The Dock makes app switching and multitasking much faster, so treat it like your actual work shelf.

Use widgets for glanceable stuff

Calendar, Reminders, Weather, Batteries, and Notes widgets can cut down on needless app hopping.

Learn one Files folder system

Even a simple structure like Projects, Personal, and Reference makes the device easier to trust.

Choose a note app and stick with it

The iPad gets better when capture is consistent. Notes, Goodnotes, Notability, or another favorite can all work.

Practice

A quick drill to make the iPad feel more fluent

Five minutes of repetition is usually enough to make a few of these feel natural.

Practice Set

Open Spotlight, launch Notes, swipe into App Switcher, place Safari beside Notes in Split View, then drag a link or sentence from Safari into Notes.

Keyboard Set

If you use an external keyboard, hold Globe to see shortcuts, then try search, app switching, copy, paste, and undo.

Memory rule

On iPad, most power comes from three places: the bottom bar, the Dock, and the Globe key.

Best mindset

Treat the iPad like a light, touch-first work machine and it starts to make a lot more sense.
